10 Insurance Company Tactics
After a Car Accident

Las Vegas, Nevada · Updated March 2026 · 10 min read

Insurance companies are not your friends after an accident. This isn't cynicism — it's business. They collect premiums and minimize payouts. Their adjusters are trained professionals who handle hundreds of claims a year. You've handled zero. That information gap is exactly what they count on.

In 2025, car insurance customers filed over 35,000 formal complaints with the National Association of Insurance Commissioners — up 7.5% from the year before. Only 37% of Americans trust that their insurer will actually help them when something goes wrong. Here's why — and what to do about each one.

People who work with an accident attorney recover on average 3.5 times more than those who negotiate alone with the insurance company. The adjuster knows this. That's why they'll tell you a lawyer "isn't necessary."

Tactic #1: The Lightning-Fast Settlement Offer

TACTIC 01
The Lowball Early Offer
What they do: An offer arrives within 24-72 hours of the accident — before you understand your injuries, before you know your rights, before you've spoken to anyone. It sounds reasonable. It's not.
Your counter: Never accept a first offer without review. Your injuries may not fully manifest for days or weeks. Once you sign, you waive all future claims. Call us before you respond to any settlement offer.

Tactic #2: The Recorded Statement Trap

TACTIC 02
The Friendly Phone Call
What they do: An adjuster calls, sounds sympathetic, and asks if they can "just record this for our files." They ask open-ended questions designed to get you to minimize your injuries, admit partial fault, or contradict your future medical records.
Your counter: You are not legally required to provide a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance. Politely decline and say your representative will be in touch. Then call us.

Tactic #3: The Broad Medical Release

TACTIC 03
Sign Here to Speed Things Up
What they do: They ask you to sign a medical release "to verify your injuries." The release is intentionally broad — giving them access to your entire medical history, including pre-existing conditions they can use to reduce your claim.
Your counter: Only authorize release of records specifically related to this accident. Never sign a blanket medical release without legal review.

Tactic #4: Shifting Blame to You

TACTIC 04
Comparative Fault Manipulation
What they do: They suggest you were partially at fault — maybe you were "following too close" or "should have seen them coming." In Nevada, if you're found 51% or more at fault, you get nothing. Even 20% reduces your payout by 20%.
Your counter: Never admit any degree of fault at the scene or on the phone. Let your documentation — photos, police report, witness statements — tell the story objectively.

Tactic #5: Claiming Your Injuries Are Pre-Existing

TACTIC 05
The Pre-Existing Condition Defense
What they do: They obtain your full medical history (see Tactic #3) and argue your neck pain, back issues, or other symptoms existed before the accident. This shifts liability off their policyholder.
Your counter: Get medical attention immediately after the accident. Proper documentation from an accident-specialized physician ties your injuries directly to the collision. We connect you with these physicians same-day.

Tactic #6: Deliberate Delays

TACTIC 06
The Waiting Game
What they do: Drag out the process with requests for additional documentation, "reviews," or transfers between adjusters. The longer you wait, the more financially pressured you become — and the more likely you are to accept a low offer just to end it.
Your counter: Document every interaction with dates and names. Unreasonable delays can constitute bad faith insurance handling in Nevada. An attorney escalates immediately when delays become patterns.

Tactic #7: Downplaying Future Medical Costs

TACTIC 07
The Current-Bills-Only Settlement
What they do: Offer to cover your current medical bills and call it fair. This ignores future treatment, ongoing physical therapy, lost earning capacity, and pain and suffering — all of which are legally compensable.
Your counter: Never settle until treatment is complete and a doctor has assessed your long-term prognosis. Future costs must be part of any settlement calculation.

Tactic #8: Denying Treatment Coverage

TACTIC 08
That's Not Medically Necessary
What they do: Dispute the necessity of treatments your doctor recommends — specialist referrals, MRIs, chiropractic care, physical therapy. This leaves you with bills the settlement doesn't cover.
Your counter: You have the right to choose your own doctors and receive medically necessary treatment. We connect you with providers who work on lien — you pay nothing until your case settles.

Tactic #9: Undervaluing Your Vehicle

TACTIC 09
The Market Value Lowball
What they do: Use proprietary valuation tools that consistently undervalue vehicles — citing condition, mileage, and "market comparables" from their own database. The number they give you is almost never what your car is actually worth.
Your counter: Get independent valuations from at least three sources: KBB, Edmunds, and local dealers. Challenge low estimates with documentation. We know how to dispute vehicle valuations effectively.

Tactic #10: Discouraging Attorney Involvement

TACTIC 10
"You Don't Need a Lawyer"
What they do: The adjuster tells you the claim is straightforward, an attorney "will just take a third of your money," and you'll get a faster resolution by handling it yourself. They say this because people without attorneys settle for dramatically less.
Your counter: You pay nothing upfront for a PI attorney — they work on contingency. And you recover 3.5× more on average. The math isn't complicated. The adjuster knows it. Now you do too.
