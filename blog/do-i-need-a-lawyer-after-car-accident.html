Do I Need a Lawyer
After a Car Accident?

Las Vegas, Nevada · Updated March 2026 · 7 min read

This is the most honest answer you'll find on the internet: it depends. Not every accident requires an attorney. Some do absolutely. Some may not. The problem is that most people don't know how to tell the difference — and the insurance company has every reason to make sure you don't find out.

Here's a straightforward breakdown.

When You Almost Certainly Need an Attorney

GET AN ATTORNEY
You Were Injured
Any injury beyond a minor scrape — neck pain, back pain, headaches, shoulder issues — warrants legal representation. Injuries that seem minor at the scene often worsen. Once you settle, you can never reopen the claim.
GET AN ATTORNEY
The Other Driver Is Disputing Fault
Contested liability cases require legal expertise. The other driver's insurance will build the strongest possible case for their client. You need someone building yours.
GET AN ATTORNEY
You've Missed Work
Lost wages and reduced earning capacity are legally recoverable. Calculating these accurately — especially future losses — requires legal and sometimes economic expertise.
GET AN ATTORNEY
The Insurance Company Is Being Difficult
Delays, lowball offers, requests for recorded statements, blame-shifting — any of these are signals that you need representation. The insurance company's behavior toward unrepresented claimants changes dramatically once an attorney is involved.
GET AN ATTORNEY
A Commercial Vehicle Was Involved
Truck accidents, rideshare accidents, and accidents involving company vehicles involve multiple insurance layers, different liability rules, and defendants with serious legal resources. Always get representation.

When You May Be Able to Handle It Yourself

CONSIDER SELF-HANDLING
Very Minor Damage Only — No Injuries
A fender bender with no injuries and clear liability — where both parties agree on what happened — may be handleable without an attorney, especially if damages are below $5,000. Still: don't sign anything without understanding what you're giving up.
CONSIDER SELF-HANDLING
You Are Clearly At Fault With No Injuries
If you rear-ended someone at low speed, there are no injuries, and you're not disputing it — your own insurance handles the claim. An attorney adds little value here for the other party's property claim.

Even in these cases: get a consultation first. Every reputable PI attorney in Las Vegas offers a free consultation. You lose nothing by asking — and you may discover your case is worth more than you think.

The 3 Myths That Cost People Money

Myth
Hiring an attorney means I keep less money
The reality: Attorney-represented claimants recover an average of 3.5× more than unrepresented ones — even after the attorney's contingency fee. The attorney keeps a percentage of a much larger number. You still come out ahead.
Myth
I can always hire an attorney later if needed
The reality: Evidence disappears. Statements you've already given can't be taken back. Settlements you've signed are permanent. The earlier an attorney is involved, the more they can do.
Myth
The insurance company will be fair if I'm honest with them
The reality: The adjuster may be genuinely pleasant. Their job is still to minimize your payout. Their employer's financial interest is directly opposed to yours. Fairness is not part of their mandate.
