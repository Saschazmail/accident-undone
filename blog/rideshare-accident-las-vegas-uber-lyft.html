in Las Vegas

Las Vegas has one of the highest rideshare usage rates in the country — millions of Uber and Lyft trips per year concentrated in a small geographic area. With that volume comes a significant number of accidents. And rideshare accidents are uniquely complicated because the insurance picture changes depending on exactly what the driver was doing at the moment of the crash.

Understanding the insurance layers is critical to protecting your claim. Here's how it works.

The Three Coverage Phases — And Why They Matter

The coverage that applies to a rideshare accident depends entirely on the driver's status in the app at the time of the crash:

Phase 1
App Off — Personal Vehicle Only
If the driver's app is off, this is treated like any other personal vehicle accident. The driver's personal auto insurance applies exclusively. Uber and Lyft provide no coverage in this phase.
Coverage: Driver's personal insurance only
Phase 2
App On — Waiting for a Ride Request
Driver has the app active but has not yet accepted a ride. This is the gray zone. Personal insurance may deny coverage because the driver was "working." Uber and Lyft provide limited contingent liability coverage in this phase — typically $50,000 per person / $100,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $25,000 for property damage.
Coverage: Limited contingent liability from Uber/Lyft
Phase 3
Ride Accepted or Passenger in Vehicle
From the moment the driver accepts a ride request until the passenger is dropped off, Uber and Lyft provide their full $1 million commercial liability policy. This is the most coverage-rich phase and covers both passengers and third parties injured in the accident.
Coverage: $1 million commercial policy from Uber/Lyft

If You Were a Passenger

If you were riding in an Uber or Lyft when the accident occurred, you are covered by the $1 million commercial policy regardless of fault — whether your driver caused the accident or another driver did. As a passenger, you bear no comparative fault.

What to do immediately:

Screenshot your ride receipt. The app records the exact time, route, and driver details. This is critical documentation. Do it before you close the app.

Report the accident through the app. Both Uber and Lyft have in-app accident reporting. Use it — but do not accept any settlement through the app's process without legal review.

Get medical attention immediately. Even if you feel okay. Document injuries from the same day.

If Another Vehicle Hit Your Rideshare

The at-fault driver's insurance is the primary source of recovery. The rideshare's commercial policy acts as secondary coverage if the at-fault driver's insurance is insufficient. In Las Vegas, where many drivers carry only minimum $25,000 coverage, the rideshare commercial policy becomes critical for serious injuries.

If You Were Driving and a Rideshare Hit You

Determine which phase the rideshare driver was in. If they had a passenger or had accepted a ride, their company's $1 million policy is available. Get the driver's rideshare company affiliation and their ride status at the time of impact — the police report often captures this.

⚠ Don't Deal With Uber or Lyft's Claims Process Alone

Both companies have dedicated claims teams whose job is to minimize payouts. They are experienced, well-resourced, and dealing with you at your most vulnerable moment. An attorney who has handled rideshare cases in Nevada knows exactly how to navigate their process — and when to escalate beyond it.

Autonomous Vehicle Accidents

Las Vegas is a testing ground for autonomous vehicles — Zoox, Waymo, and others operate in the city. Accidents involving autonomous vehicles involve the vehicle manufacturer's liability, the operator's liability, and potentially software liability. These cases are at the frontier of personal injury law and require attorneys with specific experience in this emerging area.

If you've been in an accident involving an autonomous vehicle in Las Vegas, contact us immediately. The evidence preservation requirements and insurance structures are significantly different from standard accidents.
